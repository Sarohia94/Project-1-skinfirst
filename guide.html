Maintaining a night time routine is the most important and cheapest investment you can make for yourself for long term gain, no matter your skin type.

Night

Having a night time routine is the best thing you can do for your skin. It is a chance to reset your skin and wash the day away, quite literally!

  1. Step 1:

    Wash your face with a cleanser

    Cleaning your face at the end of the day can make or break yur skin.

    Never go to to sleep with makeup on, as this on top of your sun cream (which you should be wearing!) plus the days pollution and exposure to the elements are terrible for your skins health.

  2. Step 2:

    Utilise a target serum for your specific skin problem

    i.e. fine lines, redness, pigmentation, breakouts, elasticity or medical conditions (please see a dermatologist for the later)

  3. Step 3:

    For your eyes, use a lightweight over the counter retinol with a layer of vaseline on top to seal it in

    If you have sensitive eyes then put vaseline on first followed by Retinol to prevent irritation

  4. Step 4:

    Moisturise until dewy!

    Give your skin the hydration and nutrients it needs.

    If you are in a dry environment you need thicker, richer moisturisation.

    If you are in a humid environment you need a lightweight moisturiser.

Morning

Start your day right and prepare your skin for the days challenges!

  1. Step 1:

    Wash your face to remove to remove residue from night before

    If you do are not oily and have dry skin you can skip the morning cleanse but do really wash your face with water.

    If you are oily, you can use a cleanser but avoid over washing as it can cause more oil production.

  2. Step 2:

    Use a Vitamin C serum

    Most readily available anti-oxidant on the market.

    It is the most important serum of a day time routine.

    It has been proven to help sun cream work better for you.

  3. Step 3:

    Moisturise

    If you are in a dry environment you need thicker, richer moisturisation and then sun cream.

    If you are in a humid environment you can skip the moisturiser or opt for a lightweight hydrating serum and sun cream.

Look after your skin by looking after yourself

Stay hydrated

Maintain a healthy diet

Exercise regularly

Sleep!
